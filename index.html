Plot: Guy Montag is a firefighter who lives in a lonely, isolated society where books have been outlawed by a government fearing an independent-thinking public. It is the duty of firefighters to burn any books on sight or said collections that have been reported by informants. But Montag's life changes when he meets a rebellious and weird girl.

My opinion: I read either reviews of people who loved the book or of people who hated it. I guess I’m in the middle.
It has been a really interesting book, Ray Bradbury in this Sci-Fi novel writes his impressions on what the future will be, but it’s an interesting view. The books are burned, life is simpler (is it?), and everyone has fun. I loved the arguments between Beatty and Montag, when the two of them discussed whether books were important or books were to be burnt.
So, while I was reading, I really liked it, but at the end and now that I think about the book… I don’t know. The plot and how it was developed were really original, but all the arguments were on a more complex and philosophical level. Which – while I was reading – I liked but now I realise that the characters said too many words or sentences to state always the same concepts.
And these were the dialogues between Beatty and Montag; the ones between Mildred and Montag were easier – neither Montag nor Mildred quoted important English books – and more focused on what is necessary (a house, a work) and what isn’t (books). I think I liked those much more.
Anyway, what I wanted was actions, I was always waiting for Montag to make his next move, for something to hinder him. Those parts were the most exciting ones, the ones I read with bated breath. The end is a long sequence of these absolutely exciting parts. Everything happens so fast, a sentence ends and another starts, no stops. You have to keep reading, or you will lose focus. I loved these impressions.
But how it ended… not so much. I mean– it ended in the right way, but I was left with something unsatisfied, something bitter. If the book was loud, the ending was quiet. If was just different from the book, a new scenario, new characters, everything new. I do know it is a new beginning for everything, but it’s a little too much. I was kept on a suspance mood for too long, and when it ended I was empty, I guess. It gave me no particular emotions.
